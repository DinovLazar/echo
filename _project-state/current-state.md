## Summary (plain language)
- **The wait action (Phase 4.01, D-066–D-068).** The campaign gains a fifth action: **pass a turn in place**. `GameState.wait()` advances the shared turn by one (every echo and hazard takes its next step) while present-you holds position and records a `.stay`. Holding position is **risky** — a wait runs the **same** collision check a move does, so a mover (echo or hazard) stepping onto the held tile is a death and the run restarts. A wait is refused only while won, never checks the exit, and is the single way to pass a turn (`move(.stay)` is hardened to a no-op). This is the primitive every "strategic echo" room needs: an echo can now hold a switch *for a while* and then relocate.
  - **Stored as a fifth `Direction` case** (`.stay`, offset `(0,0)` — D-067), so a recorded run stays a plain `[Direction]` everywhere (`currentRun` / `Echo.moves` / `stepBack` / `fold` / `Echo.position`). The verified engine and its ~140-method suite needed **no migration**. The tap initializer never produces `.stay`; `Direction.allCases` is now five.
  - **An explicit "Wait" control** (D-068): a fourth button in the room HUD row (Fold / Step back / Reset run / **Wait**), enabled whenever input is unlocked (not disabled at turn 0). It routes through `BoardView`'s `commitWait` (the same input-lock-guarded path a tap/swipe uses) so the wait lands with its own feedback and deferred-death handling instead of mutating `state` directly.
  - **First-pass feedback (tune on device):** a gentle in-place **"breath"** pulse on the player (a symmetric scale keyframe, distinct from the directional step squash and from a blocked move's nothing); a low, quiet, calm **C3 tick** for `.stay` (under the pentatonic step set), layered with any stepping echoes' ticks as one chord; `haptics.step()` on a survived wait, `haptics.collision()` + the §6d dissolve on a fatal one.
- **Rooms 21–25 (Phase 4.02, D-069) — the strategic / relocating-echo band.** Five new rooms in the locked v1 format grow the campaign catalog from 20 to 25:
  - **21 "Relay" (budget 1)** — one echo holds switch A, waits, relocates to switch B; one self, two doors. The wait makes the single-echo relay possible; budget 1 makes it required.
  - **22 "Two Relays" (budget 2)** — two relocating echoes interleaved (echo 1 covers doors 1 & 3, echo 2 doors 2 & 4); no enemy.
  - **23 "Patrol & Relay" (budget 2, one enemy)** — a relay echo + a static hold + a patrol bouncing an open column; read the patrol while the relay works. Enemies enter the band here.
  - **24 "Hold & Hand-off" (budget 2)** — a 2-switch AND-door held by both echoes at once, then one echo hands off to a later door.
  - **25 "Clockwork" (budget 3, two enemies)** — the oversized hard capstone: a **three-switch** AND-door, two of those echoes then relay to later doors, two out-of-phase patrols; every advance timed. The band's milestone (not the campaign finale — that becomes room 35, D-065).
  - Each room ships a solvability test through the **real** `GameState`; rooms 23 & 25 ship a negative (a naive run that dies to the patrol). Every new patrol's one-period trace is pinned.
- **Catalog grown to 25.** `Campaign.roomIDs` is now `room-01 … room-25`; Level Select naturally shows all 25 (its 4-column grid flows them), and `Campaign.next`/`firstUnsolved` walk the longer chain. Room 25 is the band capstone; room 20 "Coda" is no longer the campaign ending (D-065).
