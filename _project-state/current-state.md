## Summary (plain language)
- **The teleport mechanic (Phase 4.03, D-070–D-072).** Portals are **linked pad pairs**: stepping onto one pad lands you instantly on its partner **as part of that move** — one shared turn ticks; you end the turn on the partner. It is automatic on step (no new input verb). Echoes teleport too (a pad is a board property, so an echo replaying a step onto a pad jumps deterministically and can relay across regions); **hazards ignore pads** (patrols stay predictable — D-036). Danger is checked where you land (the partner). The partner does **not** re-fire (arrival is terminal; a `.stay` never teleports), and a move whose **resolved** landing is blocked (off-grid / wall / closed door) is a no-op.
  - **One shared rule.** A single module-level `nonisolated func resolveLanding(from:step:pads:)` (in `Echo.swift`) is the only teleport rule, used by `Echo.position`/`upcomingCells`, `GameState.move`, and `GameState.stepBack`. It is the one place position stops being "start + the sum of the offsets" and becomes a **step-by-step walk that jumps at pads** (D-071).
  - **Pad-aware engine.** `GameState` stores the room's `portals` and a derived **bidirectional `padMap`** (built once at init, debug-asserting each portal has exactly two distinct cells and shares no cell). `move(_:)` resolves the landing then runs the off-grid/wall/closed-door guards on it (you can't teleport into a blocked cell), records the **input direction** (so a replay re-derives the jump), and checks collision/switch/door/win at the landing. `stepBack()`, `Echo.position`/`upcomingCells`, `isCellHeld`, the new `upcomingCells(of:)`, and the **echo branch** of `playerCollides` are all pad-aware; the **hazard branch** and `Hazard.position` are untouched.
  - **Position gains a `pads:` parameter defaulting to empty (D-071).** With no portals the walk equals the old offset sum, so rooms 01–25 and the verified suite need **no migration** — byte-for-byte identical behaviour.
  - **Level format v2 (D-072).** A `nonisolated struct Portal { id; cells: [two] }` and an optional top-level `portals: [Portal]` field on `Level`, decoded `decodeIfPresent ?? []`. An absent key ⇒ no portals, so every v1 room JSON (rooms 01–25) is **byte-for-byte valid and unchanged** — additive only; the D-024 fields are untouched.
  - **First-pass render.** A monochrome **pad glyph** (`PadGlyph`, a `nonisolated struct: Shape` of four bracketed corners) is drawn at every portal cell, distinct from switches (circles), the exit (ring), doors (bars), and walls (solid); paired pads share the glyph so the link reads; it uses `theme.ink` at a quiet opacity — **never the accent**. The jump is instantaneous this phase; the trail aid and the death-visual echo match are made pad-aware. Marked first-pass — a later Design pass may refine it.
- **Rooms 26–30 (Phase 4.04, D-073) — the teleport band.** Five new rooms in the v2 format grow the catalog from 25 to 30:
  - **26 "Threshold" (budget 1)** — one pad pair joins two walled regions; an echo holds switch A in region A, present-you **portals** to B and exits the door A holds. The portal, clean. No enemy.
  - **27 "Two Rooms" (budget 2)** — an echo **teleports** to the far region to hold s2 while another echo holds s1 near; two door-pairs; present-you crosses d1, portals, crosses d2 to exit. Teaches echoes traverse portals. No enemy.
  - **28 "Portal & Patrol" (budget 2, one enemy)** — a patrol bounces the partner-pad column, so the teleport **landing** is timed: danger is checked where you land. Enemies enter the band here.
  - **29 "Relay Across" (budget 2)** — wait + teleport composed: one echo holds switch A, **waits**, then relocates **through a portal** to switch B in the other region (opening an early and a late door); a second echo statically opens the portal approach. The hard pre-capstone.
  - **30 "Junction" (budget 3, two enemies)** — the oversized capstone: a hub with **three pad pairs** (six portal cells) to three isolated switch-spokes; three echoes each teleport out to hold one switch of an **AND-door across three regions**; two out-of-phase patrols sweep the corridor. The band's milestone — bites and clearly steps past room 25, and deliberately rehearses room 36's portal density.
  - Each room ships a solvability test through the **real** `GameState`; rooms 28 & 30 ship a negative (a mistimed hop / a straight rush that dies to a patrol). Every new patrol's one-period trace is pinned.
- **Catalog grown to 30.** `Campaign.roomIDs` is now `room-01 … room-30`; Level Select shows all 30 (its grid flows them) and `Campaign.next`/`firstUnsolved` walk the longer chain. Room 30 is the band capstone; room 35 remains the intended campaign finale (D-065).
