## Summary (plain language)
- **The mirror mechanic (Phase 4.05, D-074–D-076).** A mirror room splits down a vertical centerline into two halves (width must be even — debug-asserted); present-you is **two bodies on one reflected input**, one confined to each half. UP/DOWN move both the same way; LEFT/RIGHT move them oppositely (they mirror across the divide). **Partial movement is the crux:** each body moves iff its target is in its own half, on the board, not a wall, and not a closed door (read at the pre-step turn, D-038) — otherwise it stays *while the other moves*, recording a `.stay` for the turn. Using asymmetric walls/doors to knock the bodies out of lockstep is how mirror rooms are solved; if neither body can move, the input is a no-op. Contact with an echo-body or hazard by **either** body dissolves **both** (land-on + cross-paths, per body, per half — D-018/D-022); switches are held by any live body or echo-body in either half, so an **AND-door can span halves** and one folded mirror echo can hold a switch on each side at once; you win the instant **both** bodies sit on their own-half exits on the same turn (one home is not a win). The wait is reused (both bodies hold — D-066). **No teleport in mirror** — mirror × teleport is room 36's phase (4.07–4.08).
  - **A separate, additive engine (D-075).** `MirrorGameState` is built beside the verified campaign `GameState` (the D-058 Echo Run precedent), reusing the pure types and the `Echo` value type — a mirror echo is a **pair of plain `Echo`s**, one per half, replaying two turn-aligned `[Direction]` streams verbatim (D-020; a blocked turn's recorded `.stay` is reproduced and doors are never re-checked at replay). `GameState`, `Echo`, `Direction`, `Hazard`, the wait/teleport behaviour, and the single-body `BoardView` path are **byte-for-byte untouched**; with no mirror rooms instantiated, nothing existing changes.
  - **One movement rule, no view copy.** `plan(for:) -> MirrorStepPlan` is the engine's single partial-movement resolution, executed by `move(_:)` **and** read by the mirror board's presentation (with the public `bodyCollides` predicate for death prediction) — a deliberate response to the Phase-4.03 lesson, where a view-side re-implementation of the engine's landing logic silently diverged. Note: the echo **cross-paths branch is live** in mirror (desync breaks the shared parity that kept it dormant in the single-body engine).
  - **Format — the additive `mirror` block (D-076).** `nonisolated struct MirrorSpec { axis ("vertical"); startRight; exitRight }` + an optional `mirror: MirrorSpec?` on `Level` (`decodeIfPresent`, `nil` in the direct init). Presence ⇒ two-body mode (the top-level `start`/`exit` are the LEFT body's); absence ⇒ a normal room, so **rooms 01–30 are byte-for-byte valid and unchanged**. The format is now "v2 + the `mirror` extension".
  - **First-pass render (Design-refinable).** A dedicated `MirrorBoardView` + `MirrorRoomView` sit beside the untouched single-body screens (`ContentView.openRoom` branches on `level.mirror`). Drawn: the **centerline divide** (a quiet ink rule — new `BoardMetrics.divideWidth`/`divideOpacity` tokens, monochrome, never the accent), both halves' contents, **two gold active-goal exits** (the existing exit treatment, twice), both live bodies (each squashes only when it actually moved, so a blocked desynced body visibly holds still), each mirror echo as two grey bodies, the trail aid per body, and **one `BoardEffectsOverlay` per half** so fold peels and death dissolves play at each body's own tiles. Controls (Fold / Step back / Reset run / Wait) ride the same input-lock discipline (D-059/D-068); solved-state persists via the engine-agnostic room id. Known first-pass simplification: the death overlay matches colliding echo-bodies by land-on only, so a (rare) mirror swap-death shows the dissolve without the swapping echo's glide — outcome correct, cameo missed.
- **Rooms 31–35 (Phase 4.06, D-077) — the mirror band.** Five new rooms in the `mirror` format grow the catalog from 30 to 35; **room 35 "Reflection" is the campaign finale** (D-065):
  - **31 "Symmetry" (budget 1)** — symmetric halves in perfect lockstep; ONE mirror echo rests on both mirrored switches at once, then both bodies walk home. The "aha": two of me, mirrored; one fold holds both sides. No enemy.
  - **32 "Break Symmetry" (budget 1)** — the exits are NOT mirror-aligned, and reflected controls preserve the bodies' column-sum, so **lockstep can never win** (a structural proof); the one-sided wall at (2,2) desyncs the pair. No enemy — the desync lesson, clean.
  - **33 "Cross-Half Hold" (budget 2)** — two asymmetric switches (one per half) hold BOTH doors as a **cross-half AND**; every block in the room is mirror-symmetric, so no single echo can rest on both, and a live holder can't also be home: two folds, one per switch.
  - **34 "Mirror & Patrol" (budget 2, two enemies)** — two out-of-phase patrols sweep row 3, one per half: the echo **recordings** are what must be timed (steering two bodies through the sweeps at once — one recording needs a mid-run wait); the final run waits out the holds and crosses.
  - **35 "Reflection" (budget 3, two enemies — the oversized capstone and CAMPAIGN FINALE)** — 10×6, the deep end: sA behind a period-2 patrol (that recording is timed), sB deep in the right half, sC reachable **only by desync** (its mirror cell is a wall, so the approach knocks the bodies out of step) — all three holding both exit doors as a 3-switch cross-half AND; patrol h1 owns the final dash's first window, so the finish is timed too (dashing one beat early dies — the pinned negative).
  - Each room ships a solvability test through the **real** `MirrorGameState` (`assertSolvesMirror` replays the verified input stream); rooms 34 & 35 ship negatives (a mistimed recording / the one-beat-early dash); all four new patrol traces are pinned.
