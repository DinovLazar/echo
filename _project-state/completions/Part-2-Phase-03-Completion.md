# Part 2 · Phase 03 · Code — The fold choreography & the death dissolve — Completion Report
**Date:** 2026-06-27 · **Outcome (one line):** folding and dying are now legible *events* — a fold lands with a hit-pause, a grid ripple, and the grey echo rewinding into the start stack; touching an echo/hazard freezes, fizzes both selves into Canvas particles under a soft red vignette, then restarts instantly.

## 1. What shipped (plain language)
The two big moments of the game stopped being instant pops and became the satisfying beats the design calls for. **Folding** a run now pauses for a beat, sends a quiet ripple across the grid, and shows the grey echo of your just-walked run peel off and rewind back to the start square. **Dying** (touching an echo or a hazard) now glides you onto the fatal tile, holds for a calm few frames, dissolves you — and the echo you touched — into a soft spray of particles under a faint red edge-glow, then snaps you back to the start to try again. Both effects work in both colour schemes (Light and Invert), are drawn with SwiftUI's built-in Canvas (no new dependencies, no SpriteKit), and change **nothing** about the game's rules — they only animate what the engine already decided.

## 3. Decisions I made during this phase
1. **The fatal step glides onto the contact tile before the freeze (added a 120 ms beat the literal §6d omits).** Why: the no-teleport core rule (CLAUDE.md/Plan §5) and §6b ("every step glides") outweigh a strict reading of §6d, and it makes the collision legible. Alternative rejected: literal freeze-at-contact (teleports the player). **Needs a decision entry: YES → logged as D-043.**
2. **The fold peel is realised as the new echo *travelling* from the run-end back to `start`, not an in-place colour fade.** Why: an in-place crossfade at `start` is swallowed by the co-located opaque player (the original implementation did exactly this and the review confirmed the beat was invisible). Alternative rejected: in-place crossfade / spatial bloom-and-return. **Needs a decision entry: YES → logged as D-044.**
3. **The killer-hazard glow pulses on the killer's *current* (held) tile, where its diamond is actually drawn** — not its post-step tile — so the "it got you" flare sits on the visible enemy (the model is held at the pre-step turn during the dissolve). §3 surface; no separate decision entry (a within-phase presentation fix). Consequence: for a moving-hazard land-on the glow/diamond sit one tile from the fizz (the player glided toward them) — acceptable; flagged for the on-device pass.
4. **The model is held at the pre-step turn for the whole death dissolve; `restartRun()` runs only at the end (deferred mutation).** Why: §6d wants the restart to be the *last* beat, so the board must show the contact frame (not the restarted start-stack) during the dissolve. Consequence: bystander echoes/hazards lag one turn for ~506 ms; input is locked during the dissolve. Surfaced here; the deferred-restart length is part of D-043.
5. **Added `Motion.Span` (raw §1c durations) and `Ease` (scalar cubic-Bézier of the §6a curves) to `Motion.swift`** so the Canvas layer (which advances by elapsed time, not a SwiftUI `Animation`) shares the handover numbers/curves exactly — no magic timing scattered in the view. §3 surface; no decision entry (additive token-layer work the prompt invited).
6. **Authored the constants the handover left open** for the ripple (max radius 2.6·cell, 0.7 peak alpha, a fainter trailing ring) and the fizz (particle base 4.5 pt, drift 0.40–0.75·cell, ~10 particles per colliding echo, slight size-shrink). All honour the binding parts of §6c/§6d (durations, curves, colours, ~14 player particles, drift-out-and-fade) and the "subtle / fizz-not-explode" intent; the unspecified geometry is my interpretation. §3 surface; no decision entry (tuning values, not approach).
7. **Kept the vignette peak at the spec'd `0.08` for *both* palettes** rather than inventing a per-mode value. Why: §6d pins `danger.red @ 0.08`; "implement to the numbers." Risk noted by review: `0.08` over light paper may read fainter than over the near-black Invert sheet — the single most likely value to need on-device tuning (promote to a per-mode `Theme` token if so). §3 surface; no decision entry (kept spec value).
8. **The throwaway debug bar's buttons were left bypassing the fold/death input lock**, with a code comment added that the *real* in-room controls (Part 2/3) must gate on `fold == nil && death == nil`. Why: plumbing the lock up to the throwaway bar (D-017, to be removed) isn't worth the coupling; mid-effect debug taps only cause cosmetic glitches, never invalid engine state. §3 surface; no decision entry.

*(All eight surfaced. The orchestrator decides which to ratify; D-043 and D-044 are the two that changed approach.)*

- **The death sequence adds a 120 ms glide** the literal §6d does not list (D-043) — total time-to-restart ~506 ms vs §6d's 386 ms. Justified by the no-teleport rule; reversible in one line if it reads wrong on device.
- **The fold peel travels** rather than fading in place (D-044) — a concrete realisation of "peels off the just-walked path," beyond a pure colour fade.
- Otherwise the in-scope work matches the brief. Out-of-scope items (audio 2.04, haptics 2.05, Settings/real-invert 2.06, deny/trail/microcopy, debug-bar removal) were **not** touched.
