## 3. Decisions I made during this phase
1. **Equal-width, container-filling chips for the action row** (the brief said "evenly spaced and centred", layout at my discretion). I made `ControlButtonStyle` fill its container width (`maxWidth: .infinity`) so the five buttons divide the strip into equal, evenly-spaced shares. Why: intrinsic-width chips with ~14 pt padding risk overflowing/clipping the longer labels ("Step back", "Reset run") on a 375 pt device; equal shares guarantee a clean, finished fit on every device. Alternative rejected: intrinsic chips in a centered `HStack` (overflow risk on small screens). **Needs a decision entry? NO** — it's an implementation detail within the brief's "discretion" allowance, recorded here.
2. **Single-line guard on the chip label** (`lineLimit(1)` + `minimumScaleFactor(0.85)`). Why: a consequence of choice #1 — equal-width chips on a narrow device could wrap a two-word label to two lines (surfaced by the adversarial review). The guard keeps the calm single-line chip on all devices. Alternative rejected: leave it (degraded look on 375 pt). **NO** entry needed.
3. **One `ButtonStyle` with a parameterised prominent variant** (`prominentFill`/`prominentLabel`) rather than a second style for the green Next. Why: keeps "a single reusable button style" literally true while letting the solved-Next reuse the same chip mechanics (press/disabled). Alternative rejected: a separate green style (duplicates the chip). **NO** entry needed.
4. **The inner view is named `Chip`, not `Body`.** Why: naming it `Body` would collide with `ButtonStyle.Body` associatedtype and force it non-private (a real compiler error I confirmed in a probe). **NO** entry needed.

The three brief-mandated decisions (flat walls, HUD promotion, green Next) are logged as **D-053 / D-054 / D-055** verbatim per the brief.
