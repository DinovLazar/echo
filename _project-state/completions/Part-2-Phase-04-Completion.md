## 1. What shipped (plain language)
Walking now sounds. Each step plays a soft, wooden percussive tick; the four directions are tuned to four notes of a consonant (pentatonic) set, with "up" higher and "down" lower. Because every echo replays your moves in lockstep, the player and each echo that steps on a given turn all tick **at the same instant** — so a turn with several echoes is heard as a chord, and a solved room replays as a little loop of percussion you composed by solving it. Folding, dying, and solving each get one restrained sound, timed to the animations added last phase. All sounds are generated in code at launch (no audio files in the repo), and a single on/off switch is in place for the Settings screen that comes later.

## 3. Decisions I made during this phase
- **Hazards are not voiced.** The step bullet enumerates "the player and each echo that stepped"; a hazard moves too but is environmental, not an echo of your moves. I ticked only the player + stepping echoes (the generative payoff is *your* rhythm). *Alternative:* tick hazards too — rejected as off-theme and muddying. **Needs a Decisions entry? NO** (it follows the prompt's enumeration; noted here for the record).
- **Fold sound fires at the hit-pause *onset* (t = 0), not at +50 ms.** The prompt says "play the fold sound when the fold fires," and the handover lists "the hit-pause onset" as a sanctioned audio beat; t = 0 gives the most immediate feedback and the warm long decay sustains over the ripple/peel. The spec-fidelity reviewer read "onset" as the *end* of the pause and flagged it; I evaluated and **kept t = 0** by design. *Alternative:* offset to +50 ms — rejected (adds latency, contradicts "when the fold fires"). **Needs a Decisions entry? NO** (judgement within the brief; comment in code makes it explicit).
- **Death/solve timing offsets live in the audio layer, read from `Motion.Span`.** `playDeath()`/`playSolve()` apply their own beat offsets (`step + deathFreeze`; `step`) rather than the caller passing a delay, so the audio aligns to the same motion tokens the visuals use ("audio and motion share one clock"). *Alternative:* schedule from `BoardView` — rejected (scatters timing). **Needs a Decisions entry? NO.**
- **Host-time scheduling for chords.** Voices are scheduled at one shared `AVAudioTime(hostTime:)` (a global clock all nodes read) with a 15 ms lead, rather than the per-node sample timeline, to guarantee flam-free simultaneity without timeline-conversion pitfalls; a missing/past render time degrades gracefully to "as soon as possible." *Alternative:* `at: nil` burst — works perceptually but less explicit about "the same audio time." **Needs a Decisions entry? NO** (an implementation detail of D-045).
- **Synthesis params (pitches, decays, peaks, master 0.75).** Chosen to the spec by ear-in-code (C4/E4/G4/A4 ticks; G3→C3 fold; A2+noise death; E4→A4→C5 solve). All are code constants, explicitly flagged for an on-device tuning pass. **Needs a Decisions entry? NO** (tunable detail, not a direction-setting choice).
