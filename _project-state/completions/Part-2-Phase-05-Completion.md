## 1. What shipped (plain language)
The phone now taps your hand in response to what you do: a light tick each time you step, a slightly heavier tap when you fold a run, a soft "error" buzz when an echo or hazard catches you and your run restarts, and a "success" tap when you reach the exit. It is built from Apple's standard system feedback, so it automatically matches the device and respects the user's system haptics setting, and it cleanly does nothing on hardware without a Taptic Engine (and in the Simulator). The taps are paired with the visuals and sounds that already ship, and a single on/off switch (default on) is ready for the Settings screen in the next phase. None of the game's rules changed.

## 3. Decisions I made during this phase
1. **Wired the haptics in `BoardView`, not `ContentView` (the brief said the opposite).** *Why:* the brief was written for Phase 1.06; the repo is at 2.04, where all gameplay input + presentation effects (motion, fold/death choreography, audio) fire from `BoardView`. Critically, a **fatal step never calls `state.move()`** — `commitMove` predicts the death via `playerCollides` and defers the mutation to `finishDeath()`→`restartRun()` — so a collision haptic driven by reading `lastMoveOutcome` after `move()` could never fire. Firing each tap in `BoardView` alongside the existing audio call is the only way to cover the deferred-death path and keep the taps synced to the audio/visuals. `ContentView` still **owns** the `HapticsManager` (`@State`) and pre-warms it, mirroring `AudioManager`. *Rejected:* the literal `ContentView` wiring (impossible for the collision tap; de-syncs the rest). **Decisions entry? YES — D-049.**
2. **Logged the decision as D-049, not the brief's "D-028".** *Why:* D-028 has existed since Phase 1.07 (step-back); the log was at D-048. Per the never-reuse-IDs convention (there is precedent in the D-028 note itself), the next free id is D-049, with the brief's stale "built ahead of motion/particles/audio" framing corrected to reality. **Decisions entry? YES — D-049 (this is it).**
3. **Added `lastMoveOutcome` even though the haptic wiring doesn't consume it.** *Why:* it is an explicit DoD deliverable and the one harness-verifiable piece of the phase; it is a clean, additive, read-only signal any future consumer that drives `move()` directly (e.g. a real in-room control) can read. The presentation layer reads its own outcome branches instead (for sync + the deferred-death path). *Rejected:* dropping it (would miss a DoD item and a useful signal). **Decisions entry? Folded into D-049.**
4. **No `CHHapticEngine`, no `CoreHaptics` import, and no capability query.** *Why:* the brief defers custom audio-synced patterns; `UIFeedbackGenerator` self-degrades to a no-op on non-haptic hardware/Simulator, so a capability gate is unnecessary surface. **Decisions entry? Folded into D-049.**
5. **Verified by actually running the real test suite, not just type-checking it.** *Why:* the DoD asks for a pass count; the repo has no committed harness, so I built a throwaway `swiftc` + `XCTest`-shim harness that compiles the *real* test files against an `-enable-testing` `ECHO` module and runs them against the real engine (67/67 methods, 734/734 assertions). This is the established `swiftc`-harness substitute prior phases used, taken one step further (run, not just type-checked). **Decisions entry? NO** — a verification-method choice; noted here.
6. **Death/win tap fires at contact, a beat before its (deliberately delayed) sound; comments corrected.** *Why:* the 2.04 audio offsets the death sound to the §6d fizz (≈ step+deathFreeze) and the solve to arrival (≈ step), while a system `UIFeedbackGenerator` tap can't be host-time scheduled — firing at contact is the better feel. The adversarial review flagged that my initial comments overstated tap–sound co-arrival; the comments are now precise. **Decisions entry? NO** — feel detail + doc fix; noted here and flagged for the on-device pass.
