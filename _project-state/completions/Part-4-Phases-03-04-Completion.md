# Part 4 · Phases 03–04 · Code — Teleport (linked pads) + Rooms 26–30 — Completion Report
**Date:** 2026-06-29 · **Outcome (one line):** the game gains a **teleport** — linked pad pairs that land you (and your echoes) instantly on a partner across the room — and the first five rooms (26–30) built on it, so a level can be two places at once.

> Combined report for the two bundled phases: **4.03** (the teleport engine + the level-format-v2 extension + a first-pass pad glyph) and **4.04** (rooms 26–30 + their tests + the grown catalog). Part A (engine) was completed and its tests made green before Part B (rooms) was authored against the real, now-with-teleport engine.

## 1. What shipped (plain language)
Stepping onto a **pad** now lands you instantly on its linked partner — as part of that one move — so two walled-off regions can be joined by a portal. Echoes teleport too (a pad is a board property), so a recorded past self can relay across regions; hazards ignore pads, so patrols stay predictable. Built on that, five new rooms (26–30) grow the campaign from 25 to 30: a clean "portal to the exit" intro, an "echoes traverse portals" room, a "patrol guards the landing pad" timing room, a "wait then teleport-relay" pre-capstone, and an oversized capstone ("Junction") with three pad pairs and an AND-door whose three switches live in three different regions. Everything is verified against the real engine headlessly; the *feel* of a teleport and how the rooms *play* are owed on the phone (this Mac still can't run the app).

## 3. Decisions I made during this phase
- **`resolveLanding` placed as a module-level `nonisolated` free function in `Echo.swift`** (the brief said "GameState.swift … your call; just one copy"). Why: the pure positional walk lives naturally beside `Echo.position`, and a `nonisolated` free function is callable from both the `@MainActor GameState` and the `nonisolated Echo`. One copy, shared by all. **NO** new Decisions entry — within D-070/D-071's "one shared resolver" latitude.
- **The death-visual echo match and the echo-trail aid were made pad-aware too** (`triggerDeath` uses `state.padMap`; the trail uses a new `GameState.upcomingCells(of:)` wrapper). Why: presentation must agree with the now-teleporting engine (otherwise a teleported echo would fizz/preview in the wrong region). Additive, presentation-only. **NO** entry.
- **Room 29 budget = 2** (D-073 left it "2 or 3, your call from solvability"). The relay echo (hold sA → wait → teleport → hold sB) + one static hold that opens the portal approach is tight at 2 (the mirror cheese-check shows `min_echoes == 2`); the relay needs **4** waits on sA for the timing to line up (found by a wait-count search). **NO** entry — within D-073's stated latitude.
- **Room 30's AND-door is a *three-switch* AND across three regions** (`heldBy:[sA,sB,sC]`, each switch in its own isolated portal-spoke), so budget 3 is genuinely tight (all three echoes must teleport out and hold at once). Uses the existing format unchanged. The three echoes' turn-1 cells are staggered so recording the later ones never collides with the banked earlier ones. **NO** entry — an authoring choice within D-073.
- **The wait action relaxes the old "budget-N needs > N open start-neighbours" deadlock rule.** Present-you can now `wait()` in place on turn 1 (safe as long as no echo lands on `start` that turn), so N echoes can each take a start-neighbour without stranding present-you. The new rooms rely on this. **NO** entry — a consequence of D-066, noted for the next author.
- **First-pass pad glyph** (bracketed corners, `theme.ink` @ 0.50, not the accent). A first guess to refine in a later Design pass, in the spirit of D-053/D-064. **NO** entry.
- **`BoardView.commitMove` made teleport-aware (a fix caught by the adversarial review).** An independent multi-agent review of the finished diff flagged that `commitMove` still computed its `target` as a raw offset sum, so on a teleporting move the *presentation* layer predicted legality/death at the pre-jump pad cell instead of the resolved partner — a teleport death would skip the §6d dissolve (mis-shown as a step), and a mover on a pad cell could wrongly trigger a death the engine doesn't have. The engine tests never caught it (they drive `GameState` directly, not the view). Fixed: `commitMove` now uses `resolveLanding(from:step:pads:)`, matching `GameState.move`; app type-check re-confirmed clean (0/0, both configs). Not reachable in the shipped rooms (no mover rests on a pad cell present-you hops from), but it would have mis-fed-back teleport deaths on device. The same review independently re-verified the engine (0 findings) and re-derived rooms 26–30 with its own engine + an exhaustive min-folds search (reference solutions win, traces match, negatives die, budgets tight, portal required). **NO** Decisions entry — a presentation correctness fix within D-070.
