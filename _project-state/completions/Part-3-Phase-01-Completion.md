## 1. What shipped (plain language)
The game went from ten teaching rooms to a **full, hand-crafted twenty-room campaign that you can actually finish**. Ten new rooms (`room-11` … `room-20`) were authored as plain JSON level files in the existing locked format and added, in order, to the play sequence so the campaign runs straight through and ends on a finale room ("Coda"). The new rooms introduce — purely through level data, with no new code — cooperating echoes (AND-doors), wider gates (multi-cell doors), rooms with two patrols at once, and rooms that need three echoes. Every room is proven solvable within its echo budget by an automated test, and the whole test suite passes green.

## 4. Deviations from the brief / spec
- **Room 13 "Wide Gate" ships at `echoBudget` 1, not the intent table's 2** — a **−1 adjustment, within the brief's explicit ±1 allowance, flagged here (no silent change).** The room's intent text itself specifies a one-echo solution ("you hold the wide gate (one echo) and time the crossing"), and the design has a **single switch**: a second echo would have nothing to hold, so it cannot make the room harder. Budget 1 is therefore the lean cap, and an automated minimum-folds check confirms the room **requires** one echo and is **not** solvable with zero. Keeping budget 2 would have made the room loosely over-budgeted (a budget-2 room trivially solvable with 1), which the brief warns against ("pick the smallest budget that makes the intended solution work").
- **Room 16 "Three" is 9×5, not the table's ~8×5** (+1 column). Three gates in series need three gate-columns plus an exit column, and budget-3 rooms require an **interior** start (an edge start deadlocks at turn 1 — see §3); placing the first gate adjacent to an interior start re-introduces the deadlock, so the gates sit at columns 3/5/7 with the exit at column 8 → width 9. The "~" envelopes are explicitly approximate; this is the only size deviation, and the room stays small and readable. Every other room matches its envelope.
- **No other deviations.** Every other room matches its intent-table budget and exercises its named mechanic; no engine, model, or format change; no existing room or test touched.
